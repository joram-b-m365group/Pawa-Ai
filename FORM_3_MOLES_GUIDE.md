## What is a Mole? (Simple Definition for Form 3)

A **mole** is a way to count very large numbers of tiny particles (atoms, molecules, ions).

**Think of it like this:**
- 1 dozen eggs = 12 eggs
- 1 mole of atoms = $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ atoms

That's **602,200,000,000,000,000,000,000 particles!**

---

## üéØ The ONE Formula You MUST Know

$$\boxed{n = \frac{m}{M}}$$

**Where:**
- $n$ = **number of moles** (mol)
- $m$ = **mass** (grams, g)
- $M$ = **molar mass** (g/mol) - found from periodic table

**In words:**
$$\text{Moles} = \frac{\text{Mass you have}}{\text{Molar mass from periodic table}}$$

---

## üìù Step-by-Step Method (Form 3 Level)

### Step 1: Find the Molar Mass

**Look at periodic table:**
- H (Hydrogen) = 1 g/mol
- C (Carbon) = 12 g/mol
- N (Nitrogen) = 14 g/mol
- O (Oxygen) = 16 g/mol
- Na (Sodium) = 23 g/mol
- Cl (Chlorine) = 35.5 g/mol
- Ca (Calcium) = 40 g/mol

**For compounds, ADD them up:**
- H‚ÇÇO = $2(1) + 16 = 18$ g/mol
- CO‚ÇÇ = $12 + 2(16) = 44$ g/mol
- NaCl = $23 + 35.5 = 58.5$ g/mol

---

### Step 2: Put Numbers in Formula

$$n = \frac{m}{M}$$

## üí° Easy Examples for Form 3

### Example 1: Water (H‚ÇÇO)

**Question:** How many moles in 36 grams of water?

**Step 1: Find molar mass**
- H = 1 g/mol, so 2H = 2 g/mol
- O = 16 g/mol
- Total: $M = 2 + 16 = 18$ g/mol

**Step 2: Write formula**
$$n = \frac{m}{M}$$

**Step 3: Substitute values**
$$n = \frac{36 \text{ g}}{18 \text{ g/mol}}$$

**Step 4: Calculate**
$$n = 2 \text{ mol}$$

**‚úÖ Answer: 36g of water = 2 moles**

---

### Example 2: Oxygen Gas (O‚ÇÇ)

**Question:** Moles in 64g of oxygen gas?

**Step 1: Molar mass**
- O‚ÇÇ means 2 oxygen atoms
- $M = 2 \times 16 = 32$ g/mol

**Step 2: Apply formula**
$$n = \frac{64}{32} = 2 \text{ mol}$$

**‚úÖ Answer: 64g O‚ÇÇ = 2 moles**

---

### Example 3: Carbon Dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ)

**Question:** How many moles in 22g of CO‚ÇÇ?

**Step 1: Molar mass**
- C = 12 g/mol
- O‚ÇÇ = $2 \times 16 = 32$ g/mol
- Total: $M = 12 + 32 = 44$ g/mol

**Step 2: Calculate**
$$n = \frac{22}{44} = 0.5 \text{ mol}$$

**‚úÖ Answer: 22g CO‚ÇÇ = 0.5 moles (half a mole)**

### Example 4: Sodium Chloride (NaCl) - Table Salt

## üîÑ Reverse: Finding Mass from Moles

**Formula:**
$$m = n \times M$$

**In words:**
$$\text{Mass} = \text{Moles} \times \text{Molar mass}$$

---

### Example 6: Find Mass

**Question:** What is the mass of 3 moles of water?

**Step 1: Molar mass of H‚ÇÇO**
$M = 18$ g/mol

**Step 2: Apply formula**
$$m = n \times M = 3 \times 18 = 54 \text{ g}$$

**‚úÖ Answer: 3 moles of water = 54 grams**

---

### Example 7: Another Mass Problem

**Question:** Mass of 0.5 moles of CO‚ÇÇ?

**Step 1: Molar mass**
$M = 44$ g/mol

**Step 2: Calculate**
$$m = 0.5 \times 44 = 22 \text{ g}$$

**‚úÖ Answer: 0.5 mol CO‚ÇÇ = 22g**

## üìä The Magic Triangle (Form 3 Trick!)

```
        m
       ___
      |   |
      | n |
      |___|
        M
```

**How to use:**
1. **Cover what you want to find**
2. **What's left shows the formula**

**Examples:**
- Cover $n$ ‚Üí see $\frac{m}{M}$ ‚Üí $n = \frac{m}{M}$
- Cover $m$ ‚Üí see $n \times M$ ‚Üí $m = n \times M$
- Cover $M$ ‚Üí see $\frac{m}{n}$ ‚Üí $M = \frac{m}{n}$

### **Common Mistakes to AVOID:**

‚ùå **Mistake 1:** Forgetting to multiply when there are subscripts
- Wrong: O‚ÇÇ = 16 g/mol
- Right: O‚ÇÇ = $2 \times 16 = 32$ g/mol

‚ùå **Mistake 2:** Using wrong formula
- Don't confuse $n = \frac{m}{M}$ with $m = n \times M$

‚ùå **Mistake 3:** No units
- Wrong: $n = 2$
- Right: $n = 2$ mol

‚ùå **Mistake 4:** Not showing working
- Always write the formula first!
- Then substitute numbers
- Then calculate
