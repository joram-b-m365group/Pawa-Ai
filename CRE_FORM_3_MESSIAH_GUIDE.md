# üìñ The Concept of the Messiah

## What is a Messiah?

**Messiah** comes from the Hebrew word **"Mashiach"** meaning **"Anointed One"**
**Christ** comes from the Greek word **"Christos"** - also meaning **"Anointed One"**

### Definition:
The Messiah is the **promised deliverer and savior** sent by God to save His people.

---

## Old Testament Messianic Prophecies

### 1. The Seed of Woman (Genesis 3:15)
> "And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers; he will crush your head, and you will strike his heel."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus was born of a woman (Mary) and defeated Satan through his death and resurrection.

---

### 2. Born of a Virgin (Isaiah 7:14)
> "Therefore the Lord himself will give you a sign: The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call him Immanuel."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus was born of the Virgin Mary (Matthew 1:18-25)

---

### 3. Born in Bethlehem (Micah 5:2)
> "But you, Bethlehem Ephrathah, though you are small among the clans of Judah, out of you will come for me one who will be ruler over Israel."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus was born in Bethlehem (Luke 2:1-7)

---

### 4. A Descendant of David (2 Samuel 7:12-13)
> "When your days are over and you rest with your ancestors, I will raise up your offspring to succeed you...I will establish the throne of his kingdom forever."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus was from the lineage of David (Matthew 1:1-17, Luke 3:23-38)

---

### 5. A Prophet Like Moses (Deuteronomy 18:15)
> "The Lord your God will raise up for you a prophet like me from among you, from your fellow Israelites. You must listen to him."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus taught with authority and performed miracles like Moses

---

### 6. Suffering Servant (Isaiah 53:3-5)
> "He was despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering, and familiar with pain...he was pierced for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus suffered and died on the cross for humanity's sins

---

### 7. Betrayed for 30 Pieces of Silver (Zechariah 11:12-13)
> "So they paid me thirty pieces of silver...the price at which they valued me."

**Fulfillment:** Judas betrayed Jesus for 30 pieces of silver (Matthew 26:14-16)

---

### 8. Hands and Feet Pierced (Psalm 22:16)
> "Dogs surround me, a pack of villains encircles me; they pierce my hands and my feet."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus was crucified - nails through hands and feet (John 20:25-27)

---

### 9. Resurrection (Psalm 16:10)
> "You will not abandon me to the realm of the dead, nor will you let your faithful one see decay."

**Fulfillment:** Jesus rose from the dead on the third day (Matthew 28:1-10)

---

## Jewish Expectations of the Messiah

The Jews expected the Messiah to be:

1. **A Political/Military Leader** - Would free Israel from Roman rule
2. **A King** - Would restore the Kingdom of David
3. **A Conqueror** - Would defeat Israel's enemies
4. **An Earthly Ruler** - Would establish an earthly kingdom

### Why Jews Rejected Jesus:
‚ùå He didn't overthrow Roman rule
‚ùå He associated with sinners and tax collectors
‚ùå He claimed to be God (they saw as blasphemy)
‚ùå He taught spiritual, not political, salvation
‚ùå He was crucified (they thought Messiah couldn't die)

# üë∂ Birth and Early Life of Jesus

## The Annunciation (Luke 1:26-38)

**Angel Gabriel appeared to Mary:**
- Told her she would conceive by the Holy Spirit
- The child would be the Son of God
- His name would be Jesus
- He would be great and reign forever

**Mary's Response:**
> "I am the Lord's servant. May your word to me be fulfilled." (Luke 1:38)

---

## The Birth of Jesus (Luke 2:1-20)

### Circumstances:
1. **Census ordered by Caesar Augustus** - Mary and Joseph traveled to Bethlehem
2. **No room in the inn** - Jesus born in a manger (animal feeding trough)
3. **Angels announced to shepherds:**
   > "Today in the town of David a Savior has been born to you; he is the Messiah, the Lord." (Luke 2:11)

4. **Shepherds visited** and worshiped the baby

---

## Visit of the Wise Men (Matthew 2:1-12)

**Magi (Wise Men) from the East:**
- Followed a star to Jerusalem
- Asked "Where is the one who has been born king of the Jews?"
- King Herod was disturbed (feared losing power)
- Found Jesus in Bethlehem
- Brought gifts: **Gold, Frankincense, Myrrh**

### Significance of Gifts:
| Gift | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Gold** | Kingship - Jesus is King |
| **Frankincense** | Deity - Jesus is God (used in temple worship) |
| **Myrrh** | Death/Burial - Jesus would die (burial spice) |

---

## Flight to Egypt (Matthew 2:13-15)

**Why?**
- Angel warned Joseph in a dream
- Herod wanted to kill Jesus
- Holy Family fled to Egypt
- Stayed until Herod died
- **Fulfilled prophecy:** "Out of Egypt I called my son" (Hosea 11:1)

---

## Massacre of the Innocents (Matthew 2:16-18)

**Herod's order:**
- Kill all boys 2 years and under in Bethlehem
- Wanted to eliminate the "rival king"
- Fulfilled Jeremiah 31:15 prophecy

---

## Jesus' Childhood in Nazareth (Luke 2:39-40)

- Family returned to Nazareth
- Jesus grew in wisdom and stature
- He was strong and filled with wisdom
- The grace of God was upon him

---

## Jesus at the Temple (Age 12) (Luke 2:41-52)

### The Event:
1. Family went to Jerusalem for Passover
2. Jesus stayed behind in the temple
3. Parents searched for 3 days
4. Found him discussing with teachers
5. Teachers amazed at his understanding

**Jesus' words to Mary:**
> "Why were you searching for me? Didn't you know I had to be in my Father's house?" (Luke 2:49)

**Then:**
- Returned to Nazareth
- Was obedient to parents
- **"Jesus grew in wisdom and stature, and in favor with God and man"** (Luke 2:52)

# üïäÔ∏è The Ministry of Jesus

## Baptism of Jesus (Matthew 3:13-17)

**By John the Baptist at River Jordan:**

1. John initially refused: "I need to be baptized by you!"
2. Jesus insisted: "It is proper for us to do this to fulfill all righteousness"
3. Jesus was baptized
4. **Heavens opened:**
   - Spirit of God descended like a dove
   - Voice from heaven: **"This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased"**

### Significance:
- Marked beginning of Jesus' public ministry
- Revealed the **Trinity** (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
- Jesus identified with humanity
- Jesus was anointed by the Holy Spirit

---

## Temptation in the Wilderness (Matthew 4:1-11)

**Led by the Spirit into the desert for 40 days:**

### The Three Temptations:

#### 1. Stones to Bread
**Satan:** "Tell these stones to become bread"
**Jesus:** "Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God" (Deuteronomy 8:3)

#### 2. Jump from Temple
**Satan:** "Throw yourself down. Angels will catch you"
**Jesus:** "Do not put the Lord your God to the test" (Deuteronomy 6:16)

#### 3. Worship Satan for Kingdoms
**Satan:** "I will give you all kingdoms if you worship me"
**Jesus:** "Away from me, Satan! Worship the Lord your God and serve him only" (Deuteronomy 6:13)

### Lessons:
- Jesus resisted temptation by quoting Scripture
- Jesus chose obedience to God over personal gain
- Jesus demonstrated he was qualified to be Savior
- Satan is real and tempts everyone

---

1. **Simon Peter** (fisherman) - "Rock" on which church built
2. **Andrew** (Peter's brother, fisherman)
3. **James** (son of Zebedee, fisherman)
4. **John** (James' brother, "beloved disciple")
5. **Philip**
6. **Bartholomew** (Nathanael)
7. **Matthew** (tax collector)
8. **Thomas** ("Doubting Thomas")
9. **James** (son of Alphaeus)
10. **Thaddaeus** (Judas, son of James)
11. **Simon the Zealot**
12. **Judas Iscariot** (betrayed Jesus)

**Jesus' call:** "Come, follow me, and I will send you out to fish for people" (Mark 1:17)

### 1. **Teaching** (Matthew 5-7, Sermon on the Mount)
- In synagogues, hillsides, by the sea
- Used parables (stories with spiritual lessons)
- Taught with authority
- Explained God's kingdom

---

### 2. **Preaching** (Mark 1:14-15)
> "The time has come. The kingdom of God has come near. Repent and believe the good news!"

Main message: **Repentance, forgiveness, God's love, eternal life**

---

### 3. **Healing the Sick**
- Blind, deaf, mute, paralyzed, lepers
- Showed compassion
- Demonstrated God's power
- Sign of Messiahship

---

### 4. **Casting Out Demons**
- Showed authority over evil
- Proof of divine power
- Satan's kingdom being destroyed

---

### 5. **Performing Miracles**
- Control over nature (calmed storms, walked on water)
- Multiplied food (fed 5000, fed 4000)
- Raised the dead

## Parables of Jesus

### Purpose of Parables:
- Simple stories with deep spiritual truths
- Relate to everyday life
- Reveal truths to those seeking
- Conceal truths from hard-hearted

---

### Key Parables:

#### 1. **The Sower** (Matthew 13:1-23)
**Lesson:** Different responses to God's Word
- Path = hardened heart
- Rocky ground = shallow faith
- Thorns = worldly distractions
- Good soil = fruitful believer

---

#### 2. **The Lost Sheep** (Luke 15:1-7)
**Lesson:** God searches for the lost
- Shepherd leaves 99 to find 1 lost sheep
- Heaven rejoices over one sinner who repents

---

#### 3. **The Prodigal Son** (Luke 15:11-32)
**Lesson:** God's forgiveness and love
- Son wastes inheritance, returns home
- Father welcomes him with celebration
- Shows God's joy when sinners repent

---

#### 4. **The Good Samaritan** (Luke 10:25-37)
**Lesson:** Love your neighbor (everyone)
- Priest and Levite passed by injured man
- Samaritan (despised) helped him
- "Go and do likewise"

---

#### 5. **The Mustard Seed** (Matthew 13:31-32)
**Lesson:** Kingdom of God starts small but grows great

---

#### 6. **The Talents** (Matthew 25:14-30)
**Lesson:** Use God's gifts wisely
- Master gave servants talents (money)
- Two invested and doubled; one buried it
- Reward for faithful use of gifts

---

#### 7. **The Wise and Foolish Builders** (Matthew 7:24-27)
**Lesson:** Build life on Jesus' teachings
- Wise man built house on rock (stands firm)
- Foolish man built on sand (collapsed)

# ‚ö° Miracles of Jesus

## Types of Miracles

### 1. **Nature Miracles** (Power over Creation)

#### Calming the Storm (Mark 4:35-41)
- Disciples panicked in boat during storm
- Jesus was sleeping
- He rebuked wind and waves: **"Quiet! Be still!"**
- Storm stopped immediately
- **Lesson:** Jesus has authority over nature

---

#### Walking on Water (Matthew 14:22-33)
- Disciples in boat, Jesus walked on water to them
- Peter walked on water toward Jesus
- Peter doubted, began to sink
- Jesus caught him: "You of little faith, why did you doubt?"
- **Lesson:** Keep faith in Jesus

---

#### Feeding 5,000 (John 6:1-15)
- 5,000+ people hungry
- Only 5 loaves and 2 fish available
- Jesus blessed and multiplied food
- Everyone ate, 12 baskets left over
- **Lesson:** Jesus provides abundantly

---

### 2. **Healing Miracles** (Power over Sickness)

#### Man Lowered Through Roof (Mark 2:1-12)
- Paralyzed man brought by 4 friends
- Crowd blocked entrance
- Friends lowered him through roof
- Jesus: "Your sins are forgiven"
- Then: "Get up, take your mat and go home"
- Man walked!
- **Lesson:** Jesus forgives sins and heals

---

#### Ten Lepers (Luke 17:11-19)
- Ten lepers called out for mercy
- Jesus told them to show themselves to priests
- All were cleansed as they went
- Only ONE returned to thank Jesus (a Samaritan)
- **Lesson:** Be grateful to God

---

#### Blind Bartimaeus (Mark 10:46-52)
- Blind beggar called out: "Jesus, Son of David, have mercy!"
- Crowd told him to be quiet
- He shouted louder
- Jesus healed him: "Your faith has healed you"
- **Lesson:** Persistent faith is rewarded

---

#### Woman with Bleeding (Mark 5:25-34)
- Suffered 12 years, spent all money on doctors
- Touched Jesus' cloak in crowd
- Instantly healed
- Jesus: "Your faith has healed you"
- **Lesson:** Faith in Jesus brings healing

---

### 3. **Exorcisms** (Power over Evil Spirits)

#### Man with Legion of Demons (Mark 5:1-20)
- Man possessed, lived in tombs
- So strong he broke chains
- Demons called themselves "Legion" (many)
- Jesus cast demons into pigs
- Pigs rushed into sea and drowned
- Man restored to right mind
- **Lesson:** Jesus has power over Satan

---

### 4. **Resurrection Miracles** (Power over Death)

#### Jairus' Daughter (Mark 5:21-43)
- Synagogue leader's daughter dying
- Before Jesus arrived, she died
- Jesus: "She is not dead but asleep"
- Took her hand: "Little girl, I say to you, get up!"
- She stood up and walked
- **Lesson:** Jesus has power over death

---

#### Widow's Son at Nain (Luke 7:11-17)
- Funeral procession for widow's only son
- Jesus had compassion
- Touched coffin: "Young man, I say to you, get up!"
- Dead man sat up and began to talk
- **Lesson:** Jesus brings life

---

#### Lazarus (John 11:1-44)
- Lazarus died, buried 4 days
- Martha: "If you had been here, my brother would not have died"
- Jesus: "I am the resurrection and the life"
- Jesus wept (shortest verse: John 11:35)
- Jesus: "Lazarus, come out!"
- Lazarus walked out, still wrapped in grave clothes
- **Lesson:** Jesus is the resurrection and the life

---

## Purpose of Miracles

1. **Prove Jesus is the Messiah/Son of God**
2. **Show God's compassion** for human suffering
3. **Demonstrate the coming Kingdom of God**
4. **Build faith** in those who witnessed them
5. **Fulfill Old Testament prophecies**
6. **Show Jesus' authority** over nature, sickness, demons, death

# ‚úùÔ∏è The Passion and Death of Jesus

## Triumphal Entry into Jerusalem (Palm Sunday) (Mark 11:1-11)

**Jesus entered Jerusalem on a donkey:**
- Crowds spread cloaks and palm branches on road
- Shouted: **"Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord!"**
- Fulfilled prophecy (Zechariah 9:9): King comes humble, riding on donkey
- Jesus came as peaceful king, not military conqueror

---

## Cleansing the Temple (Mark 11:15-19)

**Jesus overturned tables of money changers:**
- They were exploiting worshipers
- Turned house of prayer into "den of robbers"
- Showed righteous anger at injustice
- Religious leaders plotted to kill him

---

## The Last Supper (Matthew 26:17-30)

**Jesus celebrated Passover with disciples:**

### Institution of Holy Communion:

**Bread:**
> "Take and eat; this is my body." (Matthew 26:26)

**Wine:**
> "This is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins." (Matthew 26:28)

### Jesus Predicted:
1. **Betrayal:** "One of you will betray me" (Judas)
2. **Denial:** "Before the rooster crows, you will disown me three times" (Peter)

---

## Garden of Gethsemane (Matthew 26:36-46)

**Jesus prayed in agony:**

> "My Father, if it is possible, may this cup be taken from me. Yet not as I will, but as you will."

- Prayed three times
- Disciples kept falling asleep
- Jesus' sweat was like drops of blood (Luke 22:44)
- **Showed Jesus' humanity** - he dreaded suffering
- **Showed Jesus' obedience** - "not my will, but yours"

---

## Betrayal and Arrest (Matthew 26:47-56)

**Judas betrayed Jesus with a kiss:**
- Judas received 30 pieces of silver
- Led soldiers to Gethsemane
- Kissed Jesus to identify him
- Peter cut off servant's ear (Jesus healed it)
- All disciples fled

---

## The Trials of Jesus

### 1. **Religious Trials** (Before Jewish Authorities)

#### a) Before Annas (John 18:12-14)
- Former high priest
- Informal questioning

#### b) Before Caiaphas and Sanhedrin (Matthew 26:57-68)
- Sought false testimony
- Jesus remained silent
- High priest: "Are you the Messiah, the Son of God?"
- Jesus: "You have said so. But I say to all of you: from now on you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of the Mighty One"
- They accused him of **blasphemy** (claiming to be God)
- Condemned to death
- They spit on him, struck him, mocked him

#### c) Before Sanhedrin at Daybreak (Luke 22:66-71)
- Formal condemnation
- Sentenced to death

---

### 2. **Civil Trials** (Before Roman Authorities)

#### a) Before Pontius Pilate (First Time) (Luke 23:1-7)
- Jews couldn't execute, needed Roman approval
- Accused Jesus of claiming to be king (rebellion)
- Pilate: "Are you the king of the Jews?"
- Jesus: "You have said so"
- Pilate: "I find no basis for a charge against him"
- Heard Jesus was from Galilee, sent to Herod

#### b) Before Herod Antipas (Luke 23:8-12)
- Herod wanted to see a miracle
- Jesus remained silent
- Herod and soldiers mocked him
- Put royal robe on him
- Sent back to Pilate

#### c) Before Pilate (Second Time) (Luke 23:13-25)
- Pilate wanted to release Jesus
- Offered choice: release Jesus or Barabbas (murderer)
- Crowd chose Barabbas
- Pilate washed hands: "I am innocent of this man's blood"
- Ordered Jesus to be flogged and crucified

---

## The Crucifixion (Mark 15:21-41)

### The Way to Golgotha:
- Jesus carried cross
- Simon of Cyrene helped carry it
- Women wept for him

---

### At Golgotha (Place of the Skull):
1. **Offered wine mixed with myrrh** (refused - wanted to be fully conscious)

2. **Crucified between two criminals** (9 AM)

3. **Sign above head:** "THE KING OF THE JEWS"

4. **Soldiers divided his clothes**, cast lots for garment (fulfilled Psalm 22:18)

5. **People mocked him:**
   - "Save yourself! Come down from the cross!"
   - "He saved others, but he can't save himself!"

---

### The Seven Last Words of Christ:

1. **"Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing."** (Luke 23:34)

2. **"Truly I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise."** (Luke 23:43)
   - To repentant thief

3. **"Woman, here is your son...Here is your mother."** (John 19:26-27)
   - To Mary and John

4. **"My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"** (Matthew 27:46)
   - Bearing weight of world's sin

5. **"I am thirsty."** (John 19:28)

6. **"It is finished."** (John 19:30)
   - Work of salvation completed

7. **"Father, into your hands I commit my spirit."** (Luke 23:46)

---

### Signs at Death (3 PM):
1. **Darkness covered land** (noon to 3 PM)
2. **Temple curtain torn in two** (top to bottom)
   - Access to God now open to all
3. **Earth shook, rocks split**
4. **Tombs broke open**, saints raised
5. **Centurion declared:** "Surely he was the Son of God!"

---

### The Burial (Mark 15:42-47):
- **Joseph of Arimathea** (secret disciple, Sanhedrin member) asked for body
- Wrapped in linen cloth
- Placed in new tomb cut from rock
- Large stone rolled across entrance
- **Mary Magdalene and Mary mother of Jesus** watched

---

## Significance of Jesus' Death

### 1. **Atonement for Sin**
> "God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God." (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### 2. **Reconciliation with God**
> "God was reconciling the world to himself in Christ, not counting people's sins against them." (2 Corinthians 5:19)

### 3. **Redemption**
> "In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins." (Ephesians 1:7)

### 4. **Victory Over Satan**
> "Having disarmed the powers and authorities, he made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them by the cross." (Colossians 2:15)

### 5. **Demonstration of God's Love**
> "God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." (Romans 5:8)

---

# üåÖ The Resurrection and Ascension

## The Resurrection (Sunday Morning)

### The Empty Tomb (Mark 16:1-8)

**Mary Magdalene, Mary mother of James, and Salome went to tomb:**
- Brought spices to anoint Jesus' body
- Found stone rolled away
- Saw angel in white robe
- Angel: **"Don't be alarmed. You are looking for Jesus the Nazarene, who was crucified. He has risen! He is not here."**
- Told to tell disciples Jesus going to Galilee
- Women were afraid but filled with joy

---

### Appearances of Risen Jesus

#### 1. **To Mary Magdalene** (John 20:11-18)
- First to see risen Jesus
- Thought he was gardener
- Jesus: "Mary!"
- She recognized him: "Rabboni!" (Teacher)
- Jesus: "Go tell my brothers"

---

#### 2. **To Two Disciples on Emmaus Road** (Luke 24:13-35)
- Jesus walked with them, they didn't recognize him
- He explained Scriptures about the Messiah
- At dinner, he broke bread - they recognized him
- He vanished
- They returned to Jerusalem: "The Lord has risen indeed!"

---

#### 3. **To the Disciples (Thomas Absent)** (John 20:19-23)
- Evening of resurrection day
- Doors locked for fear of Jews
- Jesus appeared: "Peace be with you!"
- Showed them hands and side
- Breathed on them: "Receive the Holy Spirit"

---

#### 4. **To Thomas (Doubting Thomas)** (John 20:24-29)
- Thomas wasn't there first time
- Said: "Unless I see nail marks...I will not believe"
- Week later, Jesus appeared again
- To Thomas: "Put your finger here; see my hands. Stop doubting and believe."
- Thomas: "My Lord and my God!"
- Jesus: **"Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed"**

---

#### 5. **To Seven Disciples at Sea of Galilee** (John 21:1-14)
- Disciples fishing all night, caught nothing
- Jesus on shore (didn't recognize him)
- Jesus: "Throw net on right side"
- Caught 153 large fish, net didn't break
- John: "It is the Lord!"
- Jesus prepared breakfast for them

---

#### 6. **Peter's Restoration** (John 21:15-19)
- Jesus asked Peter 3 times: "Do you love me?"
- Peter grieved (had denied Jesus 3 times)
- Each time Jesus said: "Feed my sheep"
- Restored Peter to ministry

---

#### 7. **To 500+ Believers** (1 Corinthians 15:6)
- Largest recorded appearance

---

#### 8. **To James** (1 Corinthians 15:7)
- Jesus' brother

---

#### 9. **The Great Commission** (Matthew 28:16-20)
- Eleven disciples went to Galilee
- Jesus: **"All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."**

## The Ascension (Acts 1:3-11)

**40 days after resurrection:**

### What Happened:
1. Jesus appeared to disciples over 40 days
2. Spoke about kingdom of God
3. Commanded them to wait in Jerusalem for Holy Spirit
4. Led them to Mount of Olives (Bethany area)
5. **Lifted up into heaven** while blessing them
6. **Cloud hid him** from their sight

## Common Exam Questions

### Question Type 1: Prophecy Fulfillment
**Q:** State five Old Testament prophecies that were fulfilled by Jesus Christ. (5 marks)

**A:**
1. Born of a virgin (Isaiah 7:14) - Matthew 1:18-25
2. Born in Bethlehem (Micah 5:2) - Matthew 2:1
3. Betrayed for 30 pieces of silver (Zechariah 11:12) - Matthew 26:14-16
4. Hands and feet pierced (Psalm 22:16) - John 20:25-27
5. Resurrection (Psalm 16:10) - Matthew 28:1-10

---

### Question Type 2: Miracles
**Q:** Describe the healing of the paralyzed man who was lowered through the roof. What lessons do we learn? (8 marks)

**A:**
**Description (5 marks):**
1. Large crowd gathered where Jesus was teaching
2. Four friends brought paralyzed man on mat
3. Couldn't get through crowd, made hole in roof
4. Lowered man down in front of Jesus
5. Jesus saw their faith, said "Your sins are forgiven"
6. Teachers of law accused blasphemy
7. Jesus proved authority by saying "Get up and walk"
8. Man stood, took mat, walked out

**Lessons (3 marks):**
1. Importance of persistent faith (friends didn't give up)
2. Jesus has power to forgive sins (divine authority)
3. True friends help others reach Jesus
4. Faith brings healing (physical and spiritual)

---

### Question Type 3: Parables
**Q:** Explain the parable of the Prodigal Son and state its main lesson. (7 marks)

**A:**
**Explanation (5 marks):**
1. Younger son demanded inheritance from father
2. Left home, squandered money in wild living
3. Became poor, fed pigs, hungry
4. Decided to return home and ask to be servant
5. Father saw him coming, ran to meet him
6. Father embraced him, put robe and ring on him
7. Threw celebration feast
8. Older brother angry, refused to join
9. Father explained: "Your brother was lost and is found"

**Main Lesson (2 marks):**
- God's love and forgiveness for repentant sinners
- Heaven rejoices when sinner returns to God
- (Also: dangers of jealousy, importance of grace)

---

### Question Type 4: The Passion
**Q:** Describe the events of the Last Supper. (6 marks)

**A:**
1. Jesus celebrated Passover with twelve disciples
2. Took bread, gave thanks, broke it
3. Said "This is my body given for you"
4. Took cup of wine
5. Said "This is my blood of the new covenant, poured out for many for forgiveness of sins"
6. Predicted one would betray him (Judas)
7. Predicted Peter would deny him three times
8. Instituted Holy Communion/Lord's Supper
9. They sang a hymn and went to Mount of Olives

---

### Question Type 5: Application
**Q:** How can Christians today demonstrate that Jesus is the Messiah? (7 marks)

**A:**
1. Live holy lives that reflect Christ's character
2. Share the gospel message with others
3. Show love and compassion (feed hungry, care for sick)
4. Stand up against injustice
5. Forgive those who wrong them
6. Study and teach the Bible
7. Pray regularly and trust God
8. Join church fellowship
9. Give generously to those in need

## Study Tips for Form 3 CRE

### Memory Techniques:

1. **For Beatitudes:** Make acronym from first words
   - Poor, Mourn, Meek, Hunger, Merciful, Pure, Peacemakers, Persecuted

2. **For 12 Disciples:** Group them
   - Fishermen: Peter, Andrew, James, John
   - Tax collector: Matthew
   - Betrayer: Judas Iscariot
   - Others: Philip, Bartholomew, Thomas, James (son of Alphaeus), Thaddaeus, Simon

3. **For Seven Last Words:** Number them 1-7 and memorize in order

4. **For Parables:** Remember the main character and lesson
   - Lost Sheep = God searches for lost
   - Prodigal Son = God forgives
   - Good Samaritan = Love everyone
   - Sower = Different responses to Gospel
