# üè∫ Ancient Civilizations

## Mesopotamia (3500-500 BCE)
**"The Cradle of Civilization"**

**Location:** Between Tigris and Euphrates Rivers (modern Iraq)

**Key Achievements:**
- **Writing:** Cuneiform (wedge-shaped)
- **Code of Laws:** Hammurabi's Code (c. 1750 BCE)
- **Mathematics:** Base-60 system (60 minutes, 360 degrees)
- **Architecture:** Ziggurats (stepped pyramids)

**Major Civilizations:**
- Sumerians (3500-2000 BCE)
- Babylonians (1894-539 BCE)
- Assyrians (2500-609 BCE)

---

## Ancient Egypt (3100-30 BCE)

**Location:** Nile River Valley

**Timeline:**
- **Old Kingdom** (2686-2181 BCE) - Pyramids built
- **Middle Kingdom** (2055-1650 BCE) - Golden Age
- **New Kingdom** (1550-1077 BCE) - Empire expansion

**Key Achievements:**
- **Pyramids:** Great Pyramid of Giza (c. 2560 BCE)
- **Writing:** Hieroglyphics
- **Mummification:** Preserved bodies for afterlife
- **Calendar:** 365-day solar calendar

**Important Pharaohs:**
- Khufu (built Great Pyramid)
- Hatshepsut (female pharaoh)
- Tutankhamun (King Tut)
- Ramses II (great builder)
- Cleopatra VII (last pharaoh)

---

## Ancient Greece (800-146 BCE)

**City-States (Polis):**

**Athens:**
- Democracy (rule by people)
- Philosophy, arts, theater
- Naval power

**Sparta:**
- Military state
- Oligarchy (rule by few)
- Land power

**Key Contributions:**
- **Democracy:** First in Athens (508 BCE)
- **Philosophy:** Socrates, Plato, Aristotle
- **Science:** Pythagoras, Archimedes, Hippocrates
- **Art:** Parthenon, sculptures, pottery
- **Literature:** Homer (Iliad, Odyssey), drama

**Important Events:**
- **Persian Wars** (490-479 BCE): Greeks vs Persians
  - Battle of Marathon (490 BCE)
  - Battle of Thermopylae (480 BCE)
  - Battle of Salamis (480 BCE)
- **Peloponnesian War** (431-404 BCE): Athens vs Sparta
- **Alexander the Great** (356-323 BCE): Conquered Persian Empire

---

## Roman Empire (27 BCE - 476 CE)

**Roman Republic** (509-27 BCE):
- Senate and consuls
- Expansion through Italy
- Punic Wars vs Carthage (Hannibal)

**Roman Empire** (27 BCE - 476 CE):
- **Augustus Caesar:** First emperor (27 BCE)
- **Pax Romana:** Roman Peace (27 BCE - 180 CE)

**Key Achievements:**
- **Law:** Roman Law (basis for many modern legal systems)
- **Engineering:** Roads, aqueducts, Colosseum
- **Military:** Professional army, legions
- **Language:** Latin (basis for Romance languages)

**Important Emperors:**
- Augustus (first emperor)
- Trajan (empire's greatest extent)
- Marcus Aurelius (philosopher king)
- Constantine (legalized Christianity, 313 CE)

**Decline and Fall:**
- **Division** (395 CE): Eastern (Byzantine) and Western
- **Fall of Western Rome** (476 CE): Invaded by Germanic tribes
- **Byzantine Empire continued** until 1453

# üè∞ Medieval Period (500-1500 CE)

## Feudalism

**Social Hierarchy:**
```
King (owns all land)
     ‚Üì
Nobles/Lords (vassals to king)
     ‚Üì
Knights (vassals to lords)
     ‚Üì
Peasants/Serfs (work the land)
```

**Manor System:**

- Lords provided protection
- Peasants farmed land

---

## The Church

**Power:**
- Center of medieval life
- Education, hospitals, charity
- Owned vast lands
- Pope vs Kings (power struggles)

**Monasteries:**
- Preserved classical knowledge
- Produced manuscripts
- Centers of learning

---

## Major Events

### The Crusades (1095-1291)
**Cause:** Recapture Holy Land from Muslims

**8 Major Crusades:**
- **First Crusade** (1096-1099): Captured Jerusalem
- Later crusades mostly failed

**Effects:**
- Increased trade with East
- Cultural exchange
- Weakened feudalism
- Strengthened monarchies

---

### Black Death (1347-1353)
**Cause:** Bubonic plague from Asia

**Impact:**
- Killed 30-60% of Europe's population
- Labor shortage ‚Üí higher wages
- Weakened feudalism
- Social upheaval

---

### Hundred Years' War (1337-1453)
**Between:** England vs France

**Key Figure:** Joan of Arc (French heroine)

**Result:** French victory, end of English claims to French throne

# üé® Renaissance & Reformation (1300-1600)

## Renaissance
**Meaning:** "Rebirth" of classical learning

**Started:** Italy (Florence) c. 1300

**Key Ideas:**
- **Humanism:** Focus on human potential
- **Secularism:** Worldly, not just religious
- **Individualism:** Personal achievement

**Key Figures:**

**Art:**
- Leonardo da Vinci (Mona Lisa, Last Supper)
- Michelangelo (Sistine Chapel, David)
- Raphael (School of Athens)

**Literature:**
- Dante (Divine Comedy)
- Shakespeare (plays, sonnets)
- Machiavelli (The Prince)

**Science:**
- Copernicus (heliocentric theory)
- Galileo (telescope, astronomy)

---

## Protestant Reformation (1517)

**Causes:**
- Church corruption (selling indulgences)
- Wealth and power of clergy
- Printing press spread ideas

**Martin Luther (1483-1546):**
- **95 Theses** (1517): Criticized Church
- Excommunicated by Pope
- Founded Lutheranism

**Effects:**
- Christianity split: Catholic vs Protestant
- Religious wars
- Catholic Counter-Reformation
- Individual interpretation of Bible

**Other Reformers:**
- John Calvin (Calvinism)
- Henry VIII (Church of England)

# üåç Age of Exploration (1400-1600)

## Causes
- Search for trade routes to Asia
- Spread Christianity
- Gold and riches
- New technology (compass, astrolabe, better ships)

---

## Key Explorers

**Christopher Columbus (1492):**
- Sailed for Spain
- Reached Americas (thought it was Asia)
- Opened New World to European colonization

**Vasco da Gama (1498):**
- Portuguese
- First European sea route to India

**Ferdinand Magellan (1519-1522):**
- First circumnavigation of Earth
- (Magellan died; crew completed)

**Exploration Timeline:**
- 1492: Columbus reaches Americas
- 1498: Da Gama reaches India
- 1519-22: Magellan's circumnavigation
- 1607: Jamestown (first English settlement)

---

## Columbian Exchange

**From Americas to Europe:**
- Corn, potatoes, tomatoes, tobacco, cacao

**From Europe to Americas:**
- Wheat, rice, horses, cattle, diseases

**Impact:**
- Population growth in Europe (new foods)
- Native American population decline (diseases)
- Triangular Trade (Africa-Americas-Europe)

# üè≠ Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)

## Started in Britain

**Reasons:**
- Natural resources (coal, iron)
- Capital (money from trade)
- Labor force
- Transportation (rivers, canals)
- Stable government

---

## Key Inventions

**Textile Industry:**
- Spinning Jenny (1764) - James Hargreaves
- Water Frame (1769) - Richard Arkwright
- Power Loom (1785) - Edmund Cartwright

**Steam Power:**
- Steam Engine (improved 1769) - James Watt
- Steamboat (1807) - Robert Fulton
- Steam Locomotive (1814) - George Stephenson

**Others:**
- Cotton Gin (1793) - Eli Whitney
- Telegraph (1837) - Samuel Morse

---

## Effects

**Positive:**
- Increased production
- Lower prices
- New jobs
- Urbanization
- Middle class growth

**Negative:**
- Poor working conditions
- Child labor
- Pollution
- Overcrowded cities
- Exploitation of workers

# ‚öîÔ∏è World War I (1914-1918)

## Causes (M.A.I.N.)

**M**ilitarism - Arms race, large armies

**A**lliances - Two sides:
- Triple Entente: Britain, France, Russia
- Triple Alliance: Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy

**I**mperialism - Competition for colonies

**N**ationalism - Extreme pride in one's country

**Immediate Cause:**
- Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand (June 28, 1914)
- Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia
- Alliance system pulled in other nations

---

## Major Events

**1914:** War begins, Western Front stalemate

**1915:** Trench warfare, poison gas introduced

**1916:** Battle of Verdun, Battle of the Somme (millions die)

**1917:**
- Russian Revolution, Russia exits war
- USA enters war (April)

**1918:**
- Germany asks for armistice
- War ends November 11, 1918

---

## Technology

- Machine guns
- Tanks
- Airplanes
- Submarines (U-boats)
- Poison gas

---

## Treaty of Versailles (1919)

**Terms:**
- Germany accepts blame (War Guilt Clause)
- Pay reparations (33 billion dollars)
- Lose all colonies
- Reduce military

**Results:**
- League of Nations created
- Germany humiliated ‚Üí resentment
- Set stage for World War II

---

# üåê World War II (1939-1945)

## Causes

**Treaty of Versailles:**
- Germany's resentment

**Great Depression (1929):**
- Economic crisis worldwide

**Rise of Dictators:**
- Adolf Hitler (Germany) - Nazi Party
- Benito Mussolini (Italy) - Fascism
- Hideki Tojo (Japan) - Militarism

**Appeasement:**
- Britain, France tried to avoid war
- Let Hitler take Austria, Czechoslovakia

---

## Timeline

**1939:** Germany invades Poland (Sept 1) - War begins

**1940:**
- Germany conquers France
- Battle of Britain (air war)

**1941:**
- Germany invades Soviet Union (June)
- Pearl Harbor attack (Dec 7) - USA enters war

**1942:**
- Battles of Midway, Stalingrad (turning points)

**1943:** Italy surrenders

**1944:** D-Day invasion (June 6) - Allies invade France

**1945:**
- Germany surrenders (May 8) - V-E Day
- Atomic bombs on Hiroshima & Nagasaki (Aug 6, 9)
- Japan surrenders (Aug 15) - V-J Day

---

## Holocaust

**Definition:** Systematic genocide of 6 million Jews

**Also targeted:**
- Romani people
- Disabled
- Political opponents
- LGBTQ+ individuals

**Concentration Camps:**
- Auschwitz, Treblinka, Dachau

**"Final Solution":** Nazi plan to exterminate all Jews

---

## Results

**Death Toll:** 70-85 million people

**New Superpowers:** USA and USSR

**United Nations:** Created (1945)

**Division of Germany:** East (Soviet) and West

**Decolonization:** European empires weakened

**Nuclear Age:** Atomic weapons

# üìö Historical Skills

## Analyzing Primary Sources

**Primary Source:** Created during time period studied
- Letters, diaries, photos, artifacts, official documents

**Questions to Ask:**
1. Who created it?
2. When was it created?
3. Why was it created?
4. What is the perspective/bias?
5. What does it tell us about the time?

---

## Analyzing Secondary Sources

**Secondary Source:** Created after event by someone not there
- Textbooks, biographies, documentaries

**Evaluate:**
- Author's credentials
- Evidence used
- Bias or perspective
- Date of publication

---

## Historical Thinking

**Causation:** Why did events happen?

**Change & Continuity:** What changed? What stayed same?

**Comparison:** How are events/periods similar/different?

**Context:** What was happening at the time?

**Interpretation:** Different perspectives on same event
