# üß™ Molarity (Concentration) - Complete Guide

## What is Molarity?

**Molarity** (M) is the concentration of a solution - how much solute is dissolved in a solution.

**Definition:**
$$\boxed{M = \frac{n}{V}}$$

Where:
- $M$ = **Molarity** (mol/L or M)
- $n$ = **moles of solute** (mol)
- $V$ = **volume of solution** (liters, L)

**In words:**
$$\text{Molarity} = \frac{\text{Moles of stuff dissolved}}{\text{Liters of solution}}$$

---

## üéØ The Complete Molarity Formula

$$\boxed{M = \frac{n}{V} = \frac{m}{M_r \times V}}$$

Where:
- $M$ = Molarity (mol/L)
- $n$ = moles (mol)
- $V$ = volume (L)
- $m$ = mass (g)
- $M_r$ = molar mass (g/mol)

---

## üìê The Molarity Triangle

```
        n
       ___
      |   |
      | M |
      |___|
        V
```

**Formulas:**
- $M = \frac{n}{V}$ (Find molarity)
- $n = M \times V$ (Find moles)
- $V = \frac{n}{M}$ (Find volume)

---

## üí° Easy Examples - Step by Step

### Example 1: Basic Molarity

**Question:** If 2 moles of NaCl are dissolved in 1 liter of water, what is the molarity?

**Given:**
- $n = 2$ mol
- $V = 1$ L

**Formula:**
$$M = \frac{n}{V}$$

**Calculate:**
$$M = \frac{2 \text{ mol}}{1 \text{ L}} = 2 \text{ M}$$

**Answer: 2 M (read as "2 molar")**

---

### Example 2: From Mass

**Question:** What is the molarity of a solution containing 58.5g of NaCl in 500 mL of solution?

**Step 1:** Convert mass to moles
- Molar mass of NaCl: $M_r = 23 + 35.5 = 58.5$ g/mol
- Moles: $n = \frac{58.5}{58.5} = 1$ mol

**Step 2:** Convert mL to L
$$V = 500 \text{ mL} = 0.5 \text{ L}$$

**Step 3:** Calculate molarity
$$M = \frac{n}{V} = \frac{1 \text{ mol}}{0.5 \text{ L}} = 2 \text{ M}$$

**Answer: 2 M NaCl solution**

---

### Example 3: Finding Mass Needed

**Question:** How many grams of glucose (C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ) are needed to make 250 mL of 0.5 M solution?

**Step 1:** Convert volume to liters
$$V = 250 \text{ mL} = 0.25 \text{ L}$$

**Step 2:** Find moles needed
$$n = M \times V = 0.5 \times 0.25 = 0.125 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 3:** Find molar mass of glucose
$$M_r = 6(12) + 12(1) + 6(16) = 72 + 12 + 96 = 180 \text{ g/mol}$$

**Step 4:** Convert moles to mass
$$m = n \times M_r = 0.125 \times 180 = 22.5 \text{ g}$$

**Answer: 22.5 grams of glucose**

---

### Example 4: Dilution Problem

**Question:** If you have 100 mL of 6 M HCl, what volume of 2 M HCl can you make by diluting it?

**Use dilution formula:**
$$\boxed{M_1V_1 = M_2V_2}$$

**Given:**
- $M_1 = 6$ M (concentrated)
- $V_1 = 100$ mL
- $M_2 = 2$ M (diluted)
- $V_2 = ?$

**Calculate:**
$$6 \times 100 = 2 \times V_2$$
$$600 = 2V_2$$
$$V_2 = 300 \text{ mL}$$

**Answer: 300 mL of 2 M HCl**

## üéì Complete Worked Examples

### Example 5: Multi-Step Problem

**Question:** Calculate the molarity of H‚ÇÇSO‚ÇÑ if 49g are dissolved in 2 liters of solution.

**Step 1:** Find molar mass of H‚ÇÇSO‚ÇÑ
- H: $2 \times 1 = 2$ g/mol
- S: $1 \times 32 = 32$ g/mol
- O: $4 \times 16 = 64$ g/mol
- Total: $M_r = 2 + 32 + 64 = 98$ g/mol

**Step 2:** Convert mass to moles
$$n = \frac{m}{M_r} = \frac{49}{98} = 0.5 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 3:** Calculate molarity
$$M = \frac{n}{V} = \frac{0.5}{2} = 0.25 \text{ M}$$

**Answer: 0.25 M H‚ÇÇSO‚ÇÑ**

---

### Example 6: Finding Volume

**Question:** What volume of 3 M NaOH solution contains 1.5 moles of NaOH?

**Given:**
- $M = 3$ M
- $n = 1.5$ mol

**Formula:**
$$V = \frac{n}{M}$$

**Calculate:**
$$V = \frac{1.5 \text{ mol}}{3 \text{ M}} = 0.5 \text{ L} = 500 \text{ mL}$$

**Answer: 500 mL (or 0.5 L)**

---

### Example 7: Mixing Solutions

**Question:** If you mix 100 mL of 2 M NaCl with 200 mL of 1 M NaCl, what is the final molarity?

**Step 1:** Find moles in each solution
- Solution 1: $n_1 = M_1 \times V_1 = 2 \times 0.1 = 0.2$ mol
- Solution 2: $n_2 = M_2 \times V_2 = 1 \times 0.2 = 0.2$ mol

**Step 2:** Total moles
$$n_{total} = 0.2 + 0.2 = 0.4 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 3:** Total volume
$$V_{total} = 100 + 200 = 300 \text{ mL} = 0.3 \text{ L}$$

**Step 4:** Final molarity
$$M_{final} = \frac{0.4}{0.3} = 1.33 \text{ M}$$

**Answer: 1.33 M NaCl**

---

### Example 8: Percent to Molarity

**Question:** Convert 10% (w/v) NaOH solution to molarity.

**Note:** 10% (w/v) means 10g in 100 mL

**Step 1:** Mass in 1 L
$$m = \frac{10 \text{ g}}{100 \text{ mL}} \times 1000 \text{ mL} = 100 \text{ g}$$

**Step 2:** Molar mass of NaOH
$$M_r = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40 \text{ g/mol}$$

**Step 3:** Moles in 1 L
$$n = \frac{100}{40} = 2.5 \text{ mol}$$

**Step 4:** Molarity
$$M = \frac{2.5 \text{ mol}}{1 \text{ L}} = 2.5 \text{ M}$$

**Answer: 2.5 M NaOH**

## üìä Molarity vs Other Concentrations

| Type | Formula | Units | Example |
|------|---------|-------|---------|
| **Molarity (M)** | $\frac{n}{V}$ | mol/L | 2 M |
| **Molality (m)** | $\frac{n}{m_{solvent}}$ | mol/kg | 2 m |
| **Mass %** | $\frac{m_{solute}}{m_{solution}} \times 100$ | % | 10% |
| **Volume %** | $\frac{V_{solute}}{V_{solution}} \times 100$ | % (v/v) | 20% |
| **ppm** | $\frac{m_{solute}}{m_{solution}} \times 10^6$ | parts per million | 50 ppm |

## üî¨ Dilution Problems (Very Common!)

### The Dilution Equation:
$$\boxed{M_1V_1 = M_2V_2}$$

Where:
- $M_1$ = Initial molarity (concentrated)
- $V_1$ = Initial volume
- $M_2$ = Final molarity (diluted)
- $V_2$ = Final volume

**Key Rule:** $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$ (moles stay the same!)

---

### Example 9: Standard Dilution

**Question:** Dilute 50 mL of 12 M HCl to make 2 M solution. What is the final volume?

**Given:**
- $M_1 = 12$ M
- $V_1 = 50$ mL
- $M_2 = 2$ M
- $V_2 = ?$

**Calculate:**
$$12 \times 50 = 2 \times V_2$$
$$V_2 = \frac{600}{2} = 300 \text{ mL}$$

**How to do it:**
- Take 50 mL of 12 M HCl
- Add water until total volume = 300 mL
- Add 250 mL of water

**Answer: Final volume = 300 mL**

---

### Example 10: Water to Add

**Question:** How much water should be added to 100 mL of 5 M NaOH to make 1 M solution?

**Step 1:** Find final volume
$$M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$$
$$5 \times 100 = 1 \times V_2$$
$$V_2 = 500 \text{ mL}$$

**Step 2:** Water to add
$$\text{Water} = V_2 - V_1 = 500 - 100 = 400 \text{ mL}$$

**Answer: Add 400 mL of water**

## üéØ Common Units and Conversions

### Volume Conversions:
$$1 \text{ L} = 1000 \text{ mL}$$
$$1 \text{ mL} = 0.001 \text{ L}$$
$$500 \text{ mL} = 0.5 \text{ L}$$
$$250 \text{ mL} = 0.25 \text{ L}$$

### Molarity Units:
- M = mol/L = molar
- 1 M = 1 mol/L
- 2 M = 2 mol/L
- 0.5 M = 0.5 mol/L

## üîç Common Exam Mistakes to AVOID

‚ùå **Mistake 1:** Forgetting to convert mL ‚Üí L
- Always divide mL by 1000!
- 500 mL = 0.5 L (not 500 L!)

‚ùå **Mistake 2:** Wrong dilution formula
- Use $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$
- NOT $M_1 + V_1 = M_2 + V_2$

‚ùå **Mistake 3:** Confusing molarity with moles
- Molarity = mol/L (has units!)
- Moles = mol (different thing!)

‚ùå **Mistake 4:** No units in answer
- Always write M or mol/L
