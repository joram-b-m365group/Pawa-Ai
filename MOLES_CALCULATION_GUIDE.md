## What is a Mole?

A **mole** (mol) is a unit in chemistry that represents $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ particles (atoms, molecules, ions, etc.)

This number is called **Avogadro's Number** ($N_A$):
$$N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ particles/mol}$$

Think of it like "a dozen" = 12, but "a mole" = $6.022 \times 10^{23}$

## ğŸ¯ The Master Formula

$$n = \frac{m}{M}$$

Where:
- $n$ = number of moles (mol)
- $m$ = mass in grams (g)
- $M$ = molar mass (g/mol)

**This is the most important formula in chemistry!**

## ğŸ”¢ Step-by-Step: Finding Moles

### Method 1: From Mass (Most Common)

**Problem:** How many moles are in 18 grams of water (Hâ‚‚O)?

**Step 1:** Find the molar mass of Hâ‚‚O
- H: $1 \text{ g/mol} \times 2 = 2 \text{ g/mol}$
- O: $16 \text{ g/mol} \times 1 = 16 \text{ g/mol}$
- Total: $M = 2 + 16 = 18 \text{ g/mol}$

**Step 2:** Apply the formula
$$n = \frac{m}{M} = \frac{18 \text{ g}}{18 \text{ g/mol}} = 1 \text{ mol}$$

**Answer:** 18 grams of water = **1 mole**

---

### Method 2: From Number of Particles

**Problem:** How many moles are in $1.204 \times 10^{24}$ molecules of COâ‚‚?

**Step 1:** Use Avogadro's number
$$n = \frac{N}{N_A} = \frac{1.204 \times 10^{24}}{6.022 \times 10^{23}}$$

**Step 2:** Calculate
$$n = 2 \text{ mol}$$

**Answer:** $1.204 \times 10^{24}$ molecules = **2 moles**

---

### Method 3: From Gas Volume (at STP)

**At Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP):**
- 1 mole of any gas = 22.4 liters

**Formula:**
$$n = \frac{V}{22.4}$$

**Problem:** How many moles are in 44.8 L of oxygen gas at STP?

**Solution:**
$$n = \frac{44.8 \text{ L}}{22.4 \text{ L/mol}} = 2 \text{ mol}$$

**Answer:** 44.8 L of Oâ‚‚ at STP = **2 moles**

---

### Method 4: From Solution Concentration

**Formula:**
$$n = M \times V$$

Where:
- $M$ = molarity (mol/L)
- $V$ = volume in liters (L)

**Problem:** How many moles are in 0.5 L of 2 M NaCl solution?

**Solution:**
$$n = 2 \text{ mol/L} \times 0.5 \text{ L} = 1 \text{ mol}$$

**Answer:** 0.5 L of 2M NaCl = **1 mole**

## ğŸ¯ Quick Tips & Tricks

### Tip 1: The "18 Rule"

### Tip 2: STP Gases

### Tip 4: Empirical vs Molecular Formula
- **Empirical:** Simplest ratio (CHâ‚‚O)
- **Molecular:** Actual formula (Câ‚†Hâ‚â‚‚Oâ‚† = glucose)
- Molecular formula = $n \times$ empirical formula
