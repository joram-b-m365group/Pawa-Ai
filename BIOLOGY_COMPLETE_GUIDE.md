# ðŸ”¬ Cell Biology

## The Cell - Basic Unit of Life

### Animal vs Plant Cell

**Animal Cell Components:**
- Cell membrane (controls entry/exit)
- Nucleus (genetic material)
- Cytoplasm (jelly-like substance)
- Mitochondria (powerhouse)
- Ribosomes (protein synthesis)

**Plant Cell Additional Components:**
- Cell wall (rigid support)
- Chloroplasts (photosynthesis)
- Large vacuole (storage)

---

## Cell Division

### Mitosis (Body Cells)
**Purpose:** Growth and repair

**Stages:**
1. **Prophase** - Chromosomes condense
2. **Metaphase** - Chromosomes line up
3. **Anaphase** - Chromosomes separate
4. **Telophase** - Two nuclei form

**Result:** 2 identical daughter cells

---

### Meiosis (Sex Cells)
**Purpose:** Sexual reproduction

**Result:** 4 non-identical daughter cells with half the chromosomes

**Formula for chromosome number:**
$$\text{Diploid (2n)} \xrightarrow{\text{Meiosis}} \text{Haploid (n)}$$

**Example:** Human cells
- Body cells: $2n = 46$ chromosomes
- Sex cells (gametes): $n = 23$ chromosomes

---

## Cell Membrane Transport

### 1. Diffusion
**Definition:** Movement of particles from high to low concentration

**Rate of diffusion:**
$$\text{Rate} \propto \frac{\text{Concentration gradient} \times \text{Surface area}}{\text{Distance}}$$

**Examples:**
- Oxygen into blood: $O_2$ diffuses from lungs â†’ blood
- Carbon dioxide out: $CO_2$ diffuses from blood â†’ lungs

---

### 2. Osmosis
**Definition:** Diffusion of **water** through semi-permeable membrane

**Water potential gradient:**
$$\text{Water moves from high } \Psi \text{ to low } \Psi$$

**Effects on cells:**

**Hypertonic solution** (high solute):
- Water leaves cell
- Cell shrinks (plasmolysis)

**Hypotonic solution** (low solute):
- Water enters cell
- Cell swells (turgid)

**Isotonic solution** (equal):
- No net movement
- Cell stays same

---

### 3. Active Transport
**Definition:** Movement **against** concentration gradient (requires energy)

**Energy equation:**
$$\text{ATP} \rightarrow \text{ADP} + \text{P}_i + \text{Energy}$$

**Examples:**
- Glucose uptake in small intestine
- Mineral absorption in plant roots
- Sodium-potassium pump in neurons

# ðŸ§¬ Genetics & DNA

## DNA Structure

**Full name:** Deoxyribonucleic Acid

**Structure:** Double helix (twisted ladder)

**Components:**
- Sugar (deoxyribose)
- Phosphate group
- Nitrogenous bases

**Base pairing rules:**
$$\boxed{\text{A} \leftrightarrow \text{T} \quad \text{and} \quad \text{C} \leftrightarrow \text{G}}$$

Where:
- A = Adenine
- T = Thymine
- C = Cytosine
- G = Guanine

---

## Protein Synthesis

### Step 1: Transcription (DNA â†’ RNA)
**Location:** Nucleus

**Process:**
$$\text{DNA} \xrightarrow{\text{RNA polymerase}} \text{mRNA}$$

**Base changes:**
- DNA: A, T, C, G
- RNA: A, U, C, G (Uracil replaces Thymine)

---

### Step 2: Translation (RNA â†’ Protein)
**Location:** Ribosome

**Process:**
$$\text{mRNA} \xrightarrow{\text{tRNA + Ribosome}} \text{Protein}$$

**Genetic code:**
- 3 bases (codon) = 1 amino acid
- 20 amino acids total
- $4^3 = 64$ possible codons

**Example:**
```
DNA:  TAC-GGC-ATT-CGA
mRNA: AUG-CCG-UAA-GCU
Amino acids: Met-Pro-Stop-Ala
```

# ðŸŒ¿ Photosynthesis & Respiration

## Photosynthesis

**Overall equation:**
$$\boxed{6CO_2 + 6H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{Light + Chlorophyll}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2}$$

**In words:**
$$\text{Carbon dioxide + Water} \xrightarrow{\text{Light energy}} \text{Glucose + Oxygen}$$

---

### Light-Dependent Reactions
**Location:** Thylakoid membrane (in chloroplasts)

**Process:**
$$H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{Light}} O_2 + H^+ + e^-$$

**Products:**
- ATP (energy)
- NADPH (electron carrier)
- $O_2$ (oxygen gas)

---

### Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)
**Location:** Stroma (in chloroplasts)

**Process:**
$$CO_2 + ATP + NADPH \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6$$

---

### Factors Affecting Photosynthesis

**1. Light Intensity**
$$\text{Rate} \propto \text{Light intensity (up to saturation point)}$$

**2. COâ‚‚ Concentration**
$$\text{Rate} \propto [CO_2] \text{ (up to 0.4\%)}$$

**3. Temperature**
- Optimum: 25-35Â°C
- Below: Enzymes slow
- Above: Enzymes denature

**Limiting factor:** The factor in shortest supply limits the rate

---

## Cellular Respiration

### Aerobic Respiration (with oxygen)

**Overall equation:**
$$\boxed{C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{Energy (ATP)}}$$

**Energy yield:**
$$\text{1 glucose} \rightarrow \text{38 ATP molecules}$$

**Stages:**
1. **Glycolysis** (cytoplasm): Glucose â†’ 2 Pyruvate + 2 ATP
2. **Krebs Cycle** (mitochondria): Pyruvate â†’ $CO_2$ + ATP
3. **Electron Transport Chain** (inner membrane): $O_2$ â†’ $H_2O$ + 34 ATP

---

### Anaerobic Respiration (without oxygen)

**In animals (lactic acid fermentation):**
$$C_6H_{12}O_6 \rightarrow 2C_3H_6O_3 + \text{Energy}$$
$$\text{Glucose} \rightarrow \text{2 Lactic acid + 2 ATP}$$

**In yeast (alcoholic fermentation):**
$$C_6H_{12}O_6 \rightarrow 2C_2H_5OH + 2CO_2 + \text{Energy}$$
$$\text{Glucose} \rightarrow \text{2 Ethanol + 2 Carbon dioxide + 2 ATP}$$

**Energy comparison:**
- Aerobic: 38 ATP per glucose
- Anaerobic: 2 ATP per glucose

**Efficiency:**
$$\text{Aerobic efficiency} = \frac{38}{38} = 100\%$$
$$\text{Anaerobic efficiency} = \frac{2}{38} \approx 5.3\%$$

## Circulatory System

### Heart Rate Calculation

**Cardiac Output:**
$$\boxed{\text{Cardiac Output (CO)} = \text{Heart Rate (HR)} \times \text{Stroke Volume (SV)}}$$

**Example:**
- Heart rate: 70 beats/min
- Stroke volume: 70 mL/beat

$$CO = 70 \times 70 = 4900 \text{ mL/min} = 4.9 \text{ L/min}$$

---

### Blood Pressure

**Formula:**
$$\text{Blood Pressure} = \frac{\text{Systolic}}{\text{Diastolic}}$$

**Normal:** $\frac{120}{80}$ mmHg

**Components:**
- Systolic: Pressure during heart contraction
- Diastolic: Pressure during heart relaxation

---

## Respiratory System

### Gas Exchange

**Fick's Law of Diffusion:**
$$\text{Rate of diffusion} = \frac{A \times D \times (C_1 - C_2)}{T}$$

Where:
- $A$ = Surface area
- $D$ = Diffusion coefficient
- $C_1 - C_2$ = Concentration gradient
- $T$ = Thickness of membrane

**In lungs:**
- Alveoli surface area: $\approx 70 \text{ m}^2$
- Membrane thickness: $\approx 0.5 \mu m$

---

### Breathing Rate

**Tidal Volume:** Volume per breath $\approx 500$ mL

**Minute Ventilation:**
$$\boxed{\text{Minute Ventilation} = \text{Breathing Rate} \times \text{Tidal Volume}}$$

**Example:**
$$MV = 15 \text{ breaths/min} \times 500 \text{ mL} = 7500 \text{ mL/min} = 7.5 \text{ L/min}$$

# ðŸŒ Ecology & Environment

## Population Dynamics

### Population Growth

**Exponential growth:**
$$\boxed{N_t = N_0 \times e^{rt}}$$

Where:
- $N_t$ = Population at time $t$
- $N_0$ = Initial population
- $r$ = Growth rate
- $t$ = Time

**Example:** Bacteria doubling every hour
- $N_0 = 100$ bacteria
- $r = 0.693$ (doubling time)
- After 5 hours:

$$N_5 = 100 \times e^{0.693 \times 5} = 100 \times e^{3.465} = 3200 \text{ bacteria}$$

---

### Carrying Capacity

**Logistic growth:**
$$\boxed{N_t = \frac{K}{1 + \frac{K - N_0}{N_0} \times e^{-rt}}}$$

Where:
- $K$ = Carrying capacity
- Other terms as before

---

## Energy Flow in Ecosystems

**10% Rule:**
$$\text{Energy transfer efficiency} \approx 10\%$$

**Trophic levels:**
$$\text{Producers} \xrightarrow{10\%} \text{Primary consumers} \xrightarrow{10\%} \text{Secondary consumers}$$

**Example:** Starting with 10,000 kJ
- Producers: $10,000$ kJ
- Primary consumers: $10,000 \times 0.1 = 1,000$ kJ
- Secondary consumers: $1,000 \times 0.1 = 100$ kJ
- Tertiary consumers: $100 \times 0.1 = 10$ kJ

**Energy pyramid:**
$$\text{Energy at level } n = E_0 \times (0.1)^n$$

## Biodiversity Indices

### Simpson's Diversity Index

$$\boxed{D = 1 - \sum \left(\frac{n}{N}\right)^2}$$

Where:
- $n$ = Number of individuals of each species
- $N$ = Total number of individuals
- $D$ = Diversity (0 to 1, higher = more diverse)

**Example:**
- Species A: 50 individuals
- Species B: 30 individuals
- Species C: 20 individuals
- Total: $N = 100$

$$D = 1 - \left[\left(\frac{50}{100}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{30}{100}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{20}{100}\right)^2\right]$$
$$D = 1 - [0.25 + 0.09 + 0.04] = 1 - 0.38 = 0.62$$

# ðŸ¦´ Evolution & Classification

## Natural Selection

### Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

**Allele frequencies:**
$$\boxed{p + q = 1}$$

**Genotype frequencies:**
$$\boxed{p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1}$$

Where:
- $p$ = Frequency of dominant allele
- $q$ = Frequency of recessive allele
- $p^2$ = Homozygous dominant
- $2pq$ = Heterozygous
- $q^2$ = Homozygous recessive

**Example:**
- 64% have dominant phenotype (AA or Aa)
- 36% have recessive phenotype (aa)

$$q^2 = 0.36 \Rightarrow q = 0.6$$
$$p = 1 - 0.6 = 0.4$$

**Frequencies:**
- $AA$: $p^2 = 0.16$ (16%)
- $Aa$: $2pq = 2(0.4)(0.6) = 0.48$ (48%)
- $aa$: $q^2 = 0.36$ (36%)
